# Customer Behavior Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes customer shopping behavior using **SQL, Python, and Power BI** to uncover purchasing patterns, customer segments, and revenue insights.  
The goal is to understand customer preferences and support **data-driven business decisions** for improving marketing and sales strategies.

---

## Project Objectives
- Analyze customer purchasing patterns
- Identify high-revenue product categories
- Understand customer demographics and spending behavior
- Evaluate the impact of discounts and subscriptions on purchases
- Segment customers based on purchase history
- Build an interactive dashboard for business insights

## Data Workflow

The project follows a structured data analysis workflow:

1. Data Collection  
The dataset `shopping_behavior.csv` contains customer shopping behavior including demographics, purchase details, and product information.

2. Data Storage  
The dataset was imported into a **MySQL database** for SQL-based analysis.

3. Data Exploration (SQL)  
SQL queries were used to analyze business questions such as:
- Revenue by gender
- Discount usage patterns
- Product popularity
- Customer segmentation

4. Data Analysis (Python)  
Python was used for **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** using:
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn

5. Data Visualization  
Charts and graphs were created to identify trends and patterns.

6. Dashboard Development  
Insights were visualized using an **interactive Power BI dashboard**.

7. Business Insights  
The dashboard helps identify important trends in customer behavior.

## SQL Business Questions

1. Total revenue generated by male vs female customers  
2. Customers who used discounts but spent above the average purchase amount  
3. Top 5 products with the highest average review rating  
4. Average purchase amount comparison between shipping types  
5. Revenue comparison between subscribers and non-subscribers  
6. Products with the highest percentage of discounted purchases  
7. Customer segmentation into **New, Returning, and Loyal customers**  
8. Top 3 most purchased products within each category  
9. Subscription behavior of repeat buyers  
10. Revenue contribution by different age groups

## Key Insights

- Clothing category generates the highest revenue.
- Most customers are non-subscribers.
- Young and middle-aged customers contribute the highest revenue.
- Discounts significantly influence purchasing behavior.
- Repeat buyers show stronger engagement and loyalty.
